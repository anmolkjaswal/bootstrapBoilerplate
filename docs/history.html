History of the Internet

The birth of the internet dates as back as the 1950's, when international business machine corporations initially began the business process of creating large electronic computers. Then, in 1958, Dwight D. Eisenhower took the initiative to request startup funds in order to establish the advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA). ARPA was the first network to implement the protocol suite TCP/IP, and this was launched and went into business that very year. Soon enough, the well known internet pioneer, Robert Taylor, joins the team and excels to make major contributions towards the development to internet-related technologies. The first cross-country circuit in the ARPA network was installed in 1970. Later on, the ARPANET was terminated and thus began the creation of the world wide web by Tim Berners-Lee.
